The Crossroads Inn

A Novel of Warmth, Mystery, and the Roads That Lead Us Home

The inn at the crossroads doesn't choose its keeper. It keeps her.

Maren hung up her sword three years ago and hasn't looked back — or tried not to. When a dying cartographer bequeaths her an inn at the junction of five roads, she expects leaky roofs and stale bread. What she gets is a building that rearranges its own rooms, regulars who aren't entirely human, and a door that opens for anyone the inn decides needs saving.

There's a bard who plays songs that haven't been written yet. A merchant who pays in memories. A cat that might be a god — or might just be a cat. And a slow, steady warmth Maren hasn't felt since before the wars, growing in the walls and in the people who keep finding their way to her door.

But the crossroads remembers everyone who passes through. And some of them are looking for Maren.

Perfect for fans of Legends & Lattes and Can't Spell Treason Without Tea.
